ABOUT ME

// who.am.i

I'm Kevin Chege — a Computer Science undergraduate at Zetech University in Nairobi, Kenya, and someone who builds things that work in the real world. I started my degree in September 2024 and within a year I was teaching peers, competing internationally, and co-founding a startup. That pace is deliberate — I don't wait for permission to start.

My work lives at the intersection of software and hardware. On the software side: Python, C++, JavaScript, MySQL, OOP design, network programming. On the hardware side: Arduino, ESP32, IoT sensor integration, embedded systems, MQTT data pipelines. I'm equally comfortable writing a network scanner in Python or wiring up a sensor circuit on a breadboard.

I'm also a teacher. Since September 2025, I've been the IoT Instructor for the Itech Community Club at Zetech — designing and running hands-on training sessions for 20+ students in Arduino, ESP32, and embedded systems. Teaching forces you to truly understand something. You can't explain what you don't know. It has made me a sharper developer and a better communicator.

In 2025, I represented Zetech University at the Pan-African Robotics Competition (PARC) in Dakar, Senegal — one of Africa's most competitive student engineering events. Competing as part of Team Syntax Squad Eng in the AI League, we received a Certificate of Achievement for outstanding performance. It proved that Kenyan engineers can compete at the highest level on the continent. We just have to show up.

My long-term mission is straightforward: build profitable tech companies that solve real African problems. Agroke — my agri-tech platform targeting small-scale Kenyan farmers — is the first real bet. It started as a university assignment and is now being rebuilt into a full-stack startup.
